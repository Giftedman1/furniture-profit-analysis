## Overview
This project analyzes U.S. furniture sales data to understand why **increasing revenue does not translate into profitability**.

Despite strong and consistent sales growth, the analysis reveals that profit margins remain extremely low due to hidden inefficiencies in pricing, product mix, shipping decisions, and regional operations.

---

## Key Insights Snapshot

- **Total Orders:** 2,122  
- **Top Revenue Driver:** Consumer Segment  
- **Most Profitable Segment:** Corporate  
- **Loss-Making Category:** Tables & Bookcases  
- **Best Region:** West  
- **Loss Region:** Central  
- **Most Profitable Shipping Mode:** Standard Class  

---

## Problem Statement
Sales performance appears strong, but profitability remains weak and unstable.

The business lacks visibility into:
- Which products generate profit vs loss  
- How shipping and logistics affect margins  
- Regional inefficiencies driving losses  

Without this understanding, continued sales growth risks **increasing losses instead of improving performance**.

---

## Objectives
- Identify profit and loss drivers across products  
- Analyze impact of shipping methods on profitability  
- Evaluate regional and customer segment performance  
- Distinguish between profitable and unprofitable growth  
- Provide actionable business recommendations  

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Revenue vs Profit Disconnect
- Revenue is driven by a few large transactions  
- Profit per transaction is extremely low  
- A small number of losses wipes out gains  

---

### 2. Product Profitability
- **Tables:** High revenue but major losses  
- **Bookcases:** Also loss-making  
- **Chairs & Furnishings:** Strong profit drivers  

---

### 3. Customer Segments
- **Consumers:** Highest revenue, low margins  
- **Corporate:** Most profitable segment  
- **Home Office:** Stable but smaller contribution  

---

### 4. Shipping Performance
- **Standard Class:** Most profitable and widely used  
- **Same Day:** Low contribution, minimal impact  
- Faster shipping options increase cost pressure  

---

### 5. Regional Performance
- **West:** Highest revenue and profit  
- **South:** Strong efficiency  
- **Central:** Only loss-making region  

---

### 6. Seasonal Impact
- Peak months (Nov–Dec) drive revenue but reduce profit margins  
- High sales of low-margin products reduce overall profitability  

---

### 7. Unprofitable Growth
- Revenue increases year-over-year  
- Profit remains unstable and inconsistent  
- Growth is not financially sustainable  

---

## Recommendations
- Focus on high-margin products (Chairs & Furnishings)  
- Reduce or restructure loss-making products (Tables & Bookcases)  
- Prioritize profitable regions (West & South)  
- Implement stricter cost control in Central region  
- Use Standard Class as default shipping option  
- Avoid discount-heavy strategies during peak periods  
- Introduce **profit-based decision rules** for transactions

## Business Impact
This analysis reveals that the business is experiencing **unprofitable growth**, where increasing sales volume does not translate into financial gains.

Key risks identified:
- High-revenue products generating losses  
- Regional inefficiencies increasing operational cost  
- Shipping and pricing decisions reducing margins  

Addressing these issues can:
- Improve profit margins  
- Stabilize financial performance  
- Enable sustainable business growth  

---

## Conclusion
The business is growing in size but not in profitability.

Without stronger cost control and better product strategy, increased sales will continue to produce weak financial outcomes.

Sustainable growth requires shifting focus from **revenue alone to profit-driven decision-making**.

---

## Key Learnings
- High revenue does not guarantee profitability  
- A few loss-making transactions can offset large gains  
- Product mix and pricing strategy are critical to margins  
- Data-driven decisions are essential for sustainable growth
